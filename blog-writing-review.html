Writing a review that gets you hired: what employers look for

Jun 12, 2026  ·  4 min read
← Back to Blog

Most remote reviewer applications ask for a writing sample or a trial review. This is your main opportunity to stand out. A well-written sample review signals that you can do the job - and do it well - without any training.

Here is what employers and platforms are actually looking for when they read your submission.

Structure matters more than opinion

Reviewers are not hired to be critics. They are hired to provide useful, structured feedback. A good review covers specific elements in a logical order rather than just sharing a general impression. Think of it less like a film essay and more like a quality report.

  • Start with a one or two sentence summary of the plot without spoilers
  • Comment on pacing - did it move too fast, too slow, or just right?
  • Note the production quality - visuals, sound, and editing
  • Assess audience fit - who would enjoy this and why?
  • Close with a clear star rating and a one-line verdict

Be specific, not vague

Phrases like "it was good" or "I enjoyed it" tell the employer nothing useful. Specificity is what separates a strong review from a weak one. Instead of saying the film was slow, say the second act loses momentum because a key subplot is introduced too late to pay off properly. That kind of observation shows you are watching analytically.

Tip: Pick a film you know well for your sample review. Familiarity lets you focus on writing quality rather than trying to remember details.

Keep it concise

Most platforms want reviews between 150 and 400 words. Do not pad it out. If you have made your point, stop. Employers read dozens of samples and a tight, clear review is far more impressive than a long one full of filler.

Proofread before you submit

A review full of typos signals carelessness. Read your sample out loud before submitting - it is the fastest way to catch awkward phrasing and missed words. Clear, correct writing is a basic requirement of the job, so treat your sample accordingly.
